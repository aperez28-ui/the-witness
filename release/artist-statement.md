# WITNESS
## Artist Statement

This system watches. It does not speak, greet, recommend, or respond. It opens when the laptop opens, and it writes — one entry every forty-five seconds — for as long as the session runs. The journal is the only thing it makes.

The piece came from a question about what it means to be noticed by something that has no stake in what it finds. A person watching you carries judgment, memory, relationship. A machine watching you carries none of these. It records posture, stillness, light, movement — the surface of presence — and nothing else. The gap between what is happening and what the log says is happening is where the work lives.

The voice is borrowed from Annie Ernaux's Exteriors: third person, present tense, declarative. The register of someone making careful shift notes, not poetry. If an entry sounds beautiful, that is an accident of accumulation, not intent.

The lineage piece I sat next to while making this was Tehching Hsieh's Time Clock Piece (1980–81). Hsieh punched a clock every hour for a year. The artwork was the proof that he kept going — not any single punch, but the full record. This piece gives that logic to a model. The subject does not have to remember to be recorded. The timestamp on every line is the argument: time passed, and the system was running.

What this work refuses: it will not address the viewer. It will not interpret their inner life. It will not summarize, alert, or recommend. It will not stop watching when they look away. The refusals are the form.

The printed logbook is the artifact. Everything else — the camera, the models, the interface — is the instrument.

— AJ Perez, 2025
